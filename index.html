Mapping Tornado Occurrence and Damage (2010-2022)

Description of the Map

Introduction

Our proposal is based on the US map with visualization. On the map, there are the different states with different colors. The different colors signify the severity of tornado destruction in a given year. Darker colors indicate higher destruction and hurt from tornadoes in the states. Hovering the mouse over a state displays the amount of hurt and loss in a square. A checkbox for selecting the year is at the top of the map. Changing the year alters the map's colors.

Clicking a state displays two charts beside the map. The first chart shows the ten-year tornado count, with each column representing a year's tornado count. The x-axis shows years, and the y-axis shows the tornado count. The second chart displays annual property loss, with the x-axis for years and the y-axis for monetary loss. These charts remain constant despite year changes, showing fixed data for every state.
